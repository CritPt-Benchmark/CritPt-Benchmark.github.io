Problem setup:

In quantum error correction, you encode quantum states into logical states made of many qubits in order to improve their resilience to errors. In quantum error detection, you do the same but can only detect the presence of errors and not correct them. In this problem, we will consider a single [[4,2,2]] quantum error detection code, which encodes two logical qubits into four physical qubits, and investigate how robust logical quantum operations in this code are to quantum errors.

Our convention is that the four physical qubits in the [[4,2,2]] code are labelled 0,1,2,3. The two logical qubits are labelled A and B. The stabilizers are \(XXXX\) and \(ZZZZ\), where \(X\) and \(Z\) are Pauli matrices. The logical \(X\) and \(Z\) operators on the two qubits are \(X_A = XIXI\), \(X_B=XXII\), \(Z_A = ZZII\), \(Z_B = ZIZI\), up to multiplication by stabilizers.

We will consider different state preparation circuits consisting of controlled not \(CNOT_{ij}\) gates, where \(CNOT_{ij}\) has control qubit \(i\) and target qubit \(j\). As a simple model of quantum errors in hardware, we will suppose that each \(CNOT_{ij}\) gate in the circuit has a two qubit depolarizing error channel following it that produces one of the 15 non-identity two-qubit Paulis with equal probability \(p/15\). The probability \(p\) indicates the probability of an error in a single two-qubit gate. We will assess the logical infidelity of certain state preparation protocols as a function of the physical infidelity \(p\).

Challenge

Suppose that we prepare a logical two-qubit \(|00\rangle_{AB}\) state in the [[4,2,2]] code. To do so, we introduce an ancilla qubit, qubit 4, and use the following state preparation circuit:

\[ M_4 (CNOT_{04}) (CNOT_{34}) (CNOT_{23}) (CNOT_{10}) (CNOT_{12}) (H_1) \]

Note that this equation is written in matrix multiplication order, while the quantum operations in the circuit occur in the reverse order (from right-to-left in the above equation). \(H\) is a single-qubit Hadamard gate and \(M\) is a single-qubit measurement. The ancilla is used to detect errors in the state preparation circuit and makes the circuit fault-tolerant. If the ancilla measurement is \(|0\rangle\) (\(|1\rangle\)), the state preparation succeeds (fails).

What is the logical state fidelity of the final 2-qubit logical state at the end of the circuit as a function of two-qubit gate error rate \(p\), assuming the state is post-selected on all detectable errors in the code and on the ancilla qubit measuring \(|0\rangle\)?

  1. Step-by-Step Derivation
  • Code, target state, and circuit
  • Physical qubits 0,1,2,3 encode logical qubits A,B with stabilizers S = ⟨XXXX, ZZZZ⟩.
  • Logical Pauli representatives (up to stabilizers): X_A = X I X I, X_B = X X I I, Z_A = Z Z I I, Z_B = Z I Z I.

  • The target encoded state is the +1 eigenstate of Z_A and Z_B as well as XXXX and ZZZZ, i.e., the logical |00⟩_{AB}. In the computational basis this is the 4-qubit GHZ state |ψ_id⟩ = (|0000⟩ + |1111⟩)/√2 on qubits 0–3, which is prepared by H_1, CNOT_12, CNOT_10, CNOT_23. The ancilla (qubit 4) is then toggled by CNOT_34 and CNOT_04; ideally the ancilla ends in |0⟩ deterministically.

  • Noise model

  • After each CNOT, an independent two-qubit depolarizing error is applied: with probability p a uniformly random non-identity two-qubit Pauli is applied on that gate’s two qubits (each of the 15 possibilities with probability p/15), and with probability 1 − p nothing is applied. No other errors (the H gate and measurement are ideal).

  • Fault-tolerance and what can pass post-selection

  • We post-select on: 1) the ancilla measurement being |0⟩, and 2) the code stabilizers (XXXX, ZZZZ) returning +1 (i.e., the data is in the codespace).

  • Because the code is a [[4,2,2]] detection code, any single-qubit data error anticommutes with at least one stabilizer and is removed by post-selection. The circuit is designed so that a single two-qubit-gate fault is detected either by the stabilizers or by the ancilla with probability 1 at leading order. Consequently, after post-selection, the accepted outputs are fault-free to order p; the first non-zero contribution to logical infidelity is O(p^2).

  • Which logical errors can pass the ancilla check?

  • The ancilla measures the parity q0 ⊕ q3 (via CNOT_34 and CNOT_04). For the GHZ codeword, q0 = q3 ideally; the ancilla thus returns 0 in the absence of faults.
  • Any net physical Pauli E_data on the four data qubits that induces a logical X on exactly one of A or B (i.e., X_A or X_B, possibly times Z_A/B and stabilizers) necessarily flips the parity of q0⊕q3 deterministically to 1 and is rejected.

  • The only harmful logical class that preserves ancilla = 0 for the GHZ state is the “double X” class X_A X_B (up to stabilizers and logical Z’s). Concretely, the four physical representatives of X_A X_B modulo stabilizers are

    • X1 X2, X0 X3, Y1 Y2 Z0 Z3, Y0 Y3 Z1 Z2, and any of these multiplied by Z_A^a Z_B^b (a,b ∈ {0,1}) is still harmless for the ancilla (parity on qubits 0 and 3 stays even) but is harmful for |00⟩_{AB} (it flips both logical bits, i.e., |00⟩ → |11⟩, up to phases).

  • Pauli propagation from each fault location

  • Label the five CNOTs (and their subsequent error locations) as: 1) t1: after CNOT_12, 2) t2: after CNOT_10, 3) t3: after CNOT_23, 4) t4: after CNOT_34, 5) t5: after CNOT_04.
  • Using standard conjugation through CNOT_{c→t} (X_c → X_c X_t, Z_t → Z_c Z_t, etc.), the data-qubit part of a two-qubit Pauli error placed at each location propagates to the end as: • t1 (after CNOT_12, on qubits 1,2): if the error is P1 ⊗ Q2 with P1,Q2 ∈ {I,X,Y,Z}, then at the end on data R0 = X if P1 ∈ {X,Y}, else I; R1 = P1; R2 = Q2; R3 = X if Q2 ∈ {X,Y}, else I. • t2 (after CNOT_10, on qubits 1,0): data: P1 on qubit 1, Q0 on qubit 0; other data are I. • t3 (after CNOT_23, on qubits 2,3): data: P2 on qubit 2, Q3 on qubit 3; other data are I. • t4 (after CNOT_34, on qubits 3,4): data: P3 on qubit 3; additionally Z0 if the ancilla factor on qubit 4 is Z or Y; other data are I. • t5 (after CNOT_04, on qubits 0,4): data: P0 on qubit 0; other data are I.

Thus, a two-fault pattern at locations i<j produces a final data Pauli equal to the pointwise product of the two data Paulis above.

  • Characterizing when a two-fault net data Pauli equals X_A X_B up to Z-logicals and stabilizers
  • Let the final data letters on qubits 0,1,2,3 be L0,L1,L2,L3 ∈ {I, X, Y, Z}.

  • Membership in the X_A X_B coset (times Z_A^a Z_B^b and stabilizers) is equivalent to: 1) L1 ∈ {X,Y} and L2 ∈ {X,Y}; 2) L1 and L2 have the same Y-parity (i.e., both X or both Y); 3) L0 = L3 (both I, both X, both Y or both Z). These conditions automatically imply that the final Pauli commutes with both stabilizers XXXX and ZZZZ.

  • Enumerating the malignant two-fault pairs (those that satisfy the three conditions above and hence implement X_A X_B up to Z’s and stabilizers) We must consider all unordered pairs of distinct error locations {i,j} and count, among the 15×15 two-Pauli choices, those that give a final data Pauli in the X_A X_B coset. We also weight by the acceptance of the ancilla (when the ancilla operator includes X or Y on qubit 4 right before measurement, the Z-measurement outcome is random; we post-select on outcome 0, which gives an acceptance factor 1/2 in those subcases).

The only location pairs that can put X/Y on both qubits 1 and 2 are {t2,t3} and those involving t1 (since t1 touches both 1 and 2). All other pairs cannot create X/Y on both qubits 1 and 2 and hence cannot realize X_A X_B.

• Pair {t2, t3}: - From t2 we get L1 = P1 ∈ {X,Y} and L0 = Q0. - From t3 we get L2 = P2 ∈ {X,Y} and L3 = Q3. - To be in the desired coset we need P1 = P2 (both X or both Y): 2 choices, and Q0 = Q3: 4 choices (I, X, Y, Z). No ancilla involvement here, so full acceptance. - Count: 2 × 4 = 8 malignant pairs.

• Pair {t1, t2}: - From t1: L1 = P1, L2 = Q2, and if P1∈{X,Y} then L0 = X; if Q2∈{X,Y} then L3 = X. - From t2: multiply by P1′ on qubit 1 and Q0 on qubit 0. - To get L1,L2 both in {X,Y} with the same Y-parity and L0=L3, one solution is: P1,Q2 ∈ {X,Y} and P1 ≠ Q2 (one X, the other Y); choose at t2: P1′ = Z, Q0 = I. Then L1 = P1×Z and L2 = Q2 become both Y (or both X), and L0=L3=X. - This produces 2 malignant pairs (two choices for (P1,Q2)=(X,Y) or (Y,X)). No ancilla involvement; full acceptance.

• Pair {t1, t3}: - Symmetric to {t1, t2} by exchanging qubits 1↔2. Count: 2 malignant pairs. Full acceptance.

• Pair {t1, t4}: - From t1 with P1,Q2 ∈ {X,Y} and P1 = Q2 (both X or both Y), we get L1 = L2 and L0=L3 = X. - From t4 choose P3 = Z and Q4 ∈ {Z,Y}. This flips L3: X→Y and L0: X→Y (if Q4 ∈ {Z,Y}), making L0=L3=Y while preserving L1=L2. - There are 2 choices for P1=Q2 (X or Y) and 2 choices for Q4 (Z or Y). However, for Q4=Y the ancilla has a Y just before measurement, making the Z-measurement outcome random; we accept only half of those events. - Accepted count: 2 × [1 (Q4=Z, full acceptance) + 1/2 (Q4=Y)] = 3 malignant pairs (accepted).

• All other pairs: - {t1,t5} cannot maintain L0=L3 after modifying only qubit 0; {t2,t4}, {t2,t5}, {t3,t4}, {t3,t5}, {t4,t5} cannot create X/Y on both qubits 1 and 2. Count: 0.

  • Total accepted malignant pairs: 8 + 2 + 2 + 3 = 15.

  • From counts to the p-dependence

  • For any fixed pair of locations {i,j}, there are 15×15 equally likely two-Pauli choices; thus each malignant pair contributes probability (p/15)² to leading order.
  • Summing the 15 accepted malignant two-fault choices gives P_harm = (15/15²) p² + O(p³) = (1/15) p² + O(p³).
  • Post-selection normalization: the acceptance probability differs from 1 by O(p), so dividing by it changes P_harm only at O(p³). Therefore the conditional logical infidelity among accepted runs is 1 − F_L(p) = (1/15) p² + O(p³).

  • Ideal encoded state: |ψ_id⟩ = (|0000⟩ + |1111⟩)/√2 stabilized by XXXX and ZZZZ, and by Z_A=ZZII and Z_B=ZIZI.
  • Only X_A X_B-type logical errors (up to logical Z’s and stabilizers) pass the ancilla 0 post-selection and change |00⟩_{AB}.
  • Accepted malignant two-fault count: 15 out of 15² choices per chosen pair of CNOT locations, so P_harm(p) = (15/15²) p² + O(p³) = (1/15) p² + O(p³).
  • Hence the logical fidelity, conditioned on acceptance, is F_L(p) = 1 − (1/15) p² + O(p³).

  • All gates are ideal except the two-qubit CNOTs, which are followed by the specified two-qubit depolarizing channel.
  • Post-selection is on ancilla outcome |0⟩ and on both stabilizers +1.
  1. Final Answer: F_L(p) = 1 − (1/15) p² + O(p³).
