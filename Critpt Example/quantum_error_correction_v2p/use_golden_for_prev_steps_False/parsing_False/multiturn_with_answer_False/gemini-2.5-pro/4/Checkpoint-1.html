Problem setup:

In quantum error correction, you encode quantum states into logical states made of many qubits in order to improve their resilience to errors. In quantum error detection, you do the same but can only detect the presence of errors and not correct them. In this problem, we will consider a single [[4,2,2]] quantum error detection code, which encodes two logical qubits into four physical qubits, and investigate how robust logical quantum operations in this code are to quantum errors.

Our convention is that the four physical qubits in the [[4,2,2]] code are labelled 0,1,2,3. The two logical qubits are labelled A and B. The stabilizers are \(XXXX\) and \(ZZZZ\), where \(X\) and \(Z\) are Pauli matrices. The logical \(X\) and \(Z\) operators on the two qubits are \(X_A = XIXI\), \(X_B=XXII\), \(Z_A = ZZII\), \(Z_B = ZIZI\), up to multiplication by stabilizers.

We will consider different state preparation circuits consisting of controlled not \(CNOT_{ij}\) gates, where \(CNOT_{ij}\) has control qubit \(i\) and target qubit \(j\). As a simple model of quantum errors in hardware, we will suppose that each \(CNOT_{ij}\) gate in the circuit has a two qubit depolarizing error channel following it that produces one of the 15 non-identity two-qubit Paulis with equal probability \(p/15\). The probability \(p\) indicates the probability of an error in a single two-qubit gate. We will assess the logical infidelity of certain state preparation protocols as a function of the physical infidelity \(p\).

Checkpoint 1

Suppose that we wish to prepare a logical two-qubit GHZ state \((|00\rangle_{AB}+|11\rangle_{AB})/\sqrt{2}\) in the [[4,2,2]] code. To do so, we use the following state preparation circuit:

\[ (CNOT_{03}) (H_0) (CNOT_{21}) (H_2). \]

Note that this equation is written in matrix multiplication order, while the quantum operations in the circuit occur in the reverse order (from right-to-left in the above equation). \(H\) is a single-qubit Hadamard gate.

What is the physical state fidelity of the final physical 4-qubit state at the end of the circuit as a function of the two-qubit gate error rate \(p\)?

\[ |\psi_3\rangle = H_0 |\psi_2\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} (H_0|0000\rangle + H_0|0110\rangle) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}|000\rangle + \frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}|110\rangle \right) \]

\[ |\Psi_{ideal}\rangle = CNOT_{03} |\psi_3\rangle = \frac{1}{2} (CNOT_{03}|0000\rangle + CNOT_{03}|1000\rangle + CNOT_{03}|0110\rangle + CNOT_{03}|1110\rangle) \]
\[ |\Psi_{ideal}\rangle = \frac{1}{2} (|0000\rangle + |1001\rangle + |0110\rangle + |1111\rangle) \]

This is the pure state produced by the error-free circuit. Let \(\rho_{ideal} = |\Psi_{ideal}\rangle\langle\Psi_{ideal}|\).

Step 2: Model the Noisy Circuit

The circuit consists of four operations, two of which (\(CNOT_{21}\) and \(CNOT_{03}\)) are noisy. The single-qubit Hadamard gates are assumed to be perfect. Let \(U_{H2}, U_{C21}, U_{H0}, U_{C03}\) be the unitary operators for the gates. The error model states that each CNOT is followed by a two-qubit depolarizing channel \(\mathcal{E}\). The channel for a CNOT acting on qubits \(i, j\) is given by:

\[ \mathcal{E}_{ij}(\rho) = (1-p) \rho + \frac{p}{15} \sum_{P \in \{I,X,Y,Z\}^{\otimes 2} \setminus \{II\}} P_{ij} \rho P_{ij}^\dagger \]

The state at the output of the noisy circuit, \(\rho_{actual}\), is:

\[ \rho_{actual} = \mathcal{E}_{03}(U_{C03} U_{H0} \mathcal{E}_{21}(U_{C21} U_{H2} |0000\rangle\langle0000| U_{H2}^\dagger U_{C21}^\dagger) U_{H0}^\dagger U_{C03}^\dagger) \]

Recognizing the intermediate ideal states: \(|0000\rangle\langle0000| = |\psi_0\rangle\langle\psi_0|\), \(U_{H2}|\psi_0\rangle = |\psi_1\rangle\), and \(U_{C21}|\psi_1\rangle = |\psi_2\rangle\). So, \(U_{C21} U_{H2} |0000\rangle\langle0000| U_{H2}^\dagger U_{C21}^\dagger = |\psi_2\rangle\langle\psi_2|\). Let \(\rho_2 = |\psi_2\rangle\langle\psi_2|\). The expression becomes:

\[ \rho_{actual} = \mathcal{E}_{03}(U_{C03} U_{H0} \mathcal{E}_{21}(\rho_2) U_{H0}^\dagger U_{C03}^\dagger) \]

Step 3: Calculate the Physical Fidelity

The physical state fidelity is given by \(F = \langle\Psi_{ideal}|\rho_{actual}|\Psi_{ideal}\rangle = \text{Tr}(\rho_{ideal} \rho_{actual})\).

We can simplify the fidelity calculation by using the adjoint property of quantum channels and the covariance of the depolarizing channel. For a unitary \(U\) acting on the same qubits as the depolarizing channel \(\mathcal{E}\), we have \(\mathcal{E}(U\rho U^\dagger) = U\mathcal{E}(\rho)U^\dagger\). Also, the trace is cyclic, and \(\mathcal{E}\) is self-adjoint. Let \(\mathcal{U}_{final}(\cdot) = U_{C03} U_{H0} (\cdot) U_{H0}^\dagger U_{C03}^\dagger\). Then \(\rho_{ideal} = \mathcal{U}_{final}(\rho_2)\) and \(\rho_{actual} = \mathcal{E}_{03}(\mathcal{U}_{final}(\mathcal{E}_{21}(\rho_2)))\). Since the unitaries \(U_{C03}\) and \(U_{H0}\) act on qubits (0,3), they commute with the channel \(\mathcal{E}_{21}\) which acts on (2,1). The channel \(\mathcal{E}_{03}\) is covariant with respect to unitaries on qubits (0,3).

\[ F = \text{Tr}(\mathcal{U}_{final}(\rho_2) \mathcal{E}_{03}(\mathcal{U}_{final}(\mathcal{E}_{21}(\rho_2)))) = \text{Tr}(\mathcal{U}_{final}(\rho_2) \mathcal{U}_{final}(\mathcal{E}_{03}(\mathcal{E}_{21}(\rho_2)))) \]
\[ F = \text{Tr}(\rho_2 \mathcal{E}_{03}(\mathcal{E}_{21}(\rho_2))) \]

Since the channels \(\mathcal{E}_{03}\) and \(\mathcal{E}_{21}\) act on disjoint sets of qubits, they commute: \(\mathcal{E}_{03} \circ \mathcal{E}_{21} = \mathcal{E}_{21} \circ \mathcal{E}_{03}\).

\[ F = \text{Tr}(\rho_2 \mathcal{E}_{21}(\mathcal{E}_{03}(\rho_2))) = \langle\psi_2| \mathcal{E}_{21}(\mathcal{E}_{03}(|\psi_2\rangle\langle\psi_2|)) |\psi_2\rangle \]

This calculation reveals that the total fidelity is the fidelity of the intermediate state \(|\psi_2\rangle\) after passing through the two error channels.

The state \(|\psi_2\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|0000\rangle + |0110\rangle)\) can be written as a product state between the qubit pairs (0,3) and (1,2):

\[ |\psi_2\rangle = |00\rangle_{03} \otimes \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle_{12} + |11\rangle_{12}) \]

Let \(|\phi_{03}\rangle = |00\rangle_{03}\) and \(|\phi_{12}\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle_{12} + |11\rangle_{12})\). So \(|\psi_2\rangle=|\phi_{03}\rangle \otimes |\phi_{12}\rangle\).

The fidelity can be written as:

\[ F = \text{Tr}((|\phi_{03}\rangle\langle\phi_{03}| \otimes |\phi_{12}\rangle\langle\phi_{12}|) \cdot (\mathcal{E}_{03}(|\phi_{03}\rangle\langle\phi_{03}|) \otimes \mathcal{E}_{21}(|\phi_{12}\rangle\langle\phi_{12}|))) \]
\[ F = \text{Tr}(|\phi_{03}\rangle\langle\phi_{03}|\mathcal{E}_{03}(|\phi_{03}\rangle\langle\phi_{03}|)) \cdot \text{Tr}(|\phi_{12}\rangle\langle\phi_{12}|\mathcal{E}_{21}(|\phi_{12}\rangle\langle\phi_{12}|)) \]

This shows the total fidelity is the product of the fidelities for each subsystem. Let’s calculate them individually.

Fidelity for subsystem (0,3): Let \(F_{03} = \langle\phi_{03}|\mathcal{E}_{03}(|\phi_{03}\rangle\langle\phi_{03}|)|\phi_{03}\rangle\).

\[ F_{03} = (1-p) + \frac{p}{15} \sum_{P_{03} \neq II} |\langle\phi_{03}|P_{03}|\phi_{03}\rangle|^2 \]

We need the expectation values of the 15 two-qubit Pauli operators for the state \(|\phi_{03}\rangle=|00\rangle_{03}\). The only non-zero expectation values are for Paulis that are diagonal in the computational basis and do not flip the \(|00\rangle\) state. - \(\langle 00|Z_0I_3|00\rangle = \langle 0|Z|0\rangle\langle 0|I|0\rangle = 1\) - \(\langle 00|I_0Z_3|00\rangle = \langle 0|I|0\rangle\langle 0|Z|0\rangle = 1\) - \(\langle 00|Z_0Z_3|00\rangle = \langle 0|Z|0\rangle\langle 0|Z|0\rangle = 1\) All other 12 expectation values are zero. The sum of squares is \(1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2 = 3\).

\[ F_{03} = 1-p + \frac{p}{15}(3) = 1 - p + \frac{p}{5} = 1 - \frac{4}{5}p \]

Fidelity for subsystem (1,2): Let \(F_{12} = \langle\phi_{12}|\mathcal{E}_{21}(|\phi_{12}\rangle\langle\phi_{12}|)|\phi_{12}\rangle\).

\[ F_{12} = (1-p) + \frac{p}{15} \sum_{Q_{12} \neq II} |\langle\phi_{12}|Q_{12}|\phi_{12}\rangle|^2 \]

We need expectation values for the state \(|\phi_{12}\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle_{12} + |11\rangle_{12})\). This is a Bell state, which is stabilized by \(X_1X_2\) and \(Z_1Z_2\). - \(\langle\phi_{12}|X_1X_2|\phi_{12}\rangle = 1\) - \(\langle\phi_{12}|Z_1Z_2|\phi_{12}\rangle = 1\) - \(\langle\phi_{12}|Y_1Y_2|\phi_{12}\rangle = \langle\phi_{12}|(-iZ_1X_1)(-iZ_2X_2)|\phi_{12}\rangle = -\langle\phi_{12}|Z_1Z_2X_1X_2|\phi_{12}\rangle = -1\) All other 12 expectation values are zero. The sum of squares is \(1^2 + 1^2 + (-1)^2 = 3\).

\[ F_{12} = 1-p + \frac{p}{15}(3) = 1 - p + \frac{p}{5} = 1 - \frac{4}{5}p \]

Total Fidelity: The total physical state fidelity is the product of the individual fidelities:

\[ F = F_{03} \cdot F_{12} = \left(1 - \frac{4}{5}p\right) \left(1 - \frac{4}{5}p\right) = \left(1 - \frac{4}{5}p\right)^2 \]
\[ F(p) = 1 - \frac{8}{5}p + \frac{16}{25}p^2 \]

Final Answer: The physical state fidelity of the final 4-qubit state as a function of the two-qubit gate error rate \(p\) is \(F(p) = (1 - \frac{4}{5}p)^2 = 1 - \frac{8}{5}p + \frac{16}{25}p^2\).
