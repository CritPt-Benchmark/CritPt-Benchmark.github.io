Problem setup:

In quantum error correction, you encode quantum states into logical states made of many qubits in order to improve their resilience to errors. In quantum error detection, you do the same but can only detect the presence of errors and not correct them. In this problem, we will consider a single [[4,2,2]] quantum error detection code, which encodes two logical qubits into four physical qubits, and investigate how robust logical quantum operations in this code are to quantum errors.

Our convention is that the four physical qubits in the [[4,2,2]] code are labelled 0,1,2,3. The two logical qubits are labelled A and B. The stabilizers are \(XXXX\) and \(ZZZZ\), where \(X\) and \(Z\) are Pauli matrices. The logical \(X\) and \(Z\) operators on the two qubits are \(X_A = XIXI\), \(X_B=XXII\), \(Z_A = ZZII\), \(Z_B = ZIZI\), up to multiplication by stabilizers.

We will consider different state preparation circuits consisting of controlled not \(CNOT_{ij}\) gates, where \(CNOT_{ij}\) has control qubit \(i\) and target qubit \(j\). As a simple model of quantum errors in hardware, we will suppose that each \(CNOT_{ij}\) gate in the circuit has a two qubit depolarizing error channel following it that produces one of the 15 non-identity two-qubit Paulis with equal probability \(p/15\). The probability \(p\) indicates the probability of an error in a single two-qubit gate. We will assess the logical infidelity of certain state preparation protocols as a function of the physical infidelity \(p\).

Checkpoint 1

Suppose that we wish to prepare a logical two-qubit GHZ state \((|00\rangle_{AB}+|11\rangle_{AB})/\sqrt{2}\) in the [[4,2,2]] code. To do so, we use the following state preparation circuit:

\[ (CNOT_{03}) (H_0) (CNOT_{21}) (H_2). \]

Note that this equation is written in matrix multiplication order, while the quantum operations in the circuit occur in the reverse order (from right-to-left in the above equation). \(H\) is a single-qubit Hadamard gate.

What is the physical state fidelity of the final physical 4-qubit state at the end of the circuit as a function of the two-qubit gate error rate \(p\)?

Step-by-Step Derivation

1. Ideal circuit and target state

We begin in |0000⟩ on qubits (0,1,2,3). The perfect (error‐free) gates

\[ H_2,\;\;CNOT_{2\to1},\;\;H_0,\;\;CNOT_{0\to3} \]

produce the 4-qubit pure state

\[ |\psi\>\;=\;\Bigl(CNOT_{0\to3}\,H_0\,CNOT_{2\to1}\,H_2\Bigr)\,\ket{0000} \;=\;|\Phi^+\>_{0,3}\;\otimes\;|\Phi^+\>_{2,1}\,, \]

where

\[ |\Phi^+\> \;=\;\frac{\ket{00}+\ket{11}}{\sqrt2}\,. \]

2. Error model

Each CNOT gate is followed immediately by a two-qubit depolarizing channel on its two qubits. Denote by \(\Lambda_{ij}\) the channel acting on qubits \(i,j\):

\[ \Lambda_{ij}(\rho) =(1-p)\,\rho \;+\;\frac{p}{15}\sum_{\substack{P\in\{I,X,Y,Z\}^{\!\otimes2}\\P\neq I\otimes I}} P\,\rho\,P\,. \]

No other errors are assumed.

Thus the actual final state is

\[ \rho \;=\; \Lambda_{0,3}\!\Bigl(\Lambda_{2,1}\bigl(|\psi\>\<\psi|\bigr)\Bigr)\,. \]

3. Fidelity definition

The physical state fidelity is

\[ F(p)\;=\;\<\psi\,|\,\rho\,|\,\psi\>\;. \]

4. Factorization over disjoint error channels

Since

\[ |\psi\>=|\Phi^+\>_{0,3}\otimes|\Phi^+\>_{2,1}, \]

and the channels \(\Lambda_{2,1}\) and \(\Lambda_{0,3}\) act on disjoint qubit pairs, the fidelity factorizes:

\[ F(p) \;=\; F_{2,1}(p)\;\times\;F_{0,3}(p), \]

where

\[ F_{i,j}(p) =\;\<\Phi^+|\;\Lambda_{i,j}\bigl(|\Phi^+\>\<\Phi^+|\bigr)\;|\Phi^+\>. \]

5. Fidelity for one Bell pair under two-qubit depolarizing noise

Fix one pair, say qubits \((i,j)\), prepared in \(|\Phi^+\>\). Then

\[ \Lambda_{ij}(|\Phi^+\>\<\Phi^+|) =(1-p)\,|\Phi^+\>\<\Phi^+| \;+\;\frac{p}{15}\sum_{P\neq I\otimes I}P\,|\Phi^+\>\<\Phi^+|\,P. \]

Hence

\[ F_{ij}(p) =\<(1-p)\,|\Phi^+\>\<\Phi^+| \;+\;\frac{p}{15}\sum_{P\neq I\otimes I}P\,|\Phi^+\>\<\Phi^+|\,P\>. \]

Only those Pauli errors \(P\) in the set \(\{X\otimes X,\;Y\otimes Y,\;Z\otimes Z\}\) leave \(|\Phi^+\>\) invariant (up to phase), so

\[ \<\Phi^+|P|\Phi^+\> = 1 \quad\text{for }P\in\{XX,\,YY,\,ZZ\}, \]

and vanishes for the other 12 non-identity Paulis. Therefore

\[ F_{ij}(p) = (1-p)\;+\;\frac{p}{15}\times 3 =1-p+\frac{3p}{15} =1-\frac{4}{5}\,p. \]

6. Total 4-qubit fidelity

Each of the two CNOTs contributes the same factor, so

\[ F(p) = \Bigl(1-\tfrac{4}{5}\,p\Bigr)\times\Bigl(1-\tfrac{4}{5}\,p\Bigr) =\bigl(1-\tfrac{4}{5}p\bigr)^{2}. \]

Final Answer:

\[ F_{\rm phys}(p)\;=\;\bigl(1-\tfrac45\,p\bigr)^{2}\,. \]

Assistant #4

Step-by-Step Derivation

  1. Error model recap
    We apply two CNOTs, each followed by an independent two‐qubit depolarizing channel on its control–target pair \((i,j)\):
\[ \Lambda_{ij}(\rho) =(1-p)\,\rho \;+\;\frac{p}{15}\sum_{P\neq I\otimes I}P\,\rho\,P \]

where the sum runs over the 15 non‐identity two‐qubit Paulis on qubits \(i,j\).

  1. Undetectable errors
    A Pauli error \(E\) on qubits \((i,j)\) is undetectable iff it commutes with both code stabilizers \(X_0X_1X_2X_3\) and \(Z_0Z_1Z_2Z_3\). Since an error on \((2,1)\) commutes trivially with \(X_0,X_3,Z_0,Z_3\), the condition reduces to
\[ [E,\;X_1X_2]=0 \quad\text{and}\quad [E,\;Z_1Z_2]=0. \]

The only two‐qubit Paulis on \((1,2)\) that satisfy this are

\[ E\in\{\,I\otimes I,\;X_1X_2,\;Y_1Y_2,\;Z_1Z_2\}\,. \]

Each occurs with total probability

\[ \Pr\{E\in\mathrm{undetectable}\} = (1-p)\;+\;\frac{3\,p}{15} =1-\frac{4}{5}p \]

and similarly for errors on \((0,3)\).

  1. Post‐selection (no syndrome)
    We only keep runs in which both CNOT errors are undetectable. Hence the pass probability is
\[ P_{\rm pass} =\bigl(1-\tfrac{4}{5}p\bigr)^2. \]
  1. Logical vs.\ stabilizer components
    In the code’s Pauli‐normalizer, any undetectable error on \((i,j)\) can be written uniquely as
\[ E = S\cdot L, \]

where \(S\) lies in the stabilizer group (acts trivially on the logical qubits) and \(L\) is one of the four logical Paulis \(\{I,X_A,X_B,Z_A,X_AX_B,\dots\}\).
Since stabilizers have weight \(4\), the only way a pair of undetectable errors

\[ E_{21}\in\{I,\,X_1X_2,\,Y_1Y_2,\,Z_1Z_2\}, \quad E_{03}\in\{I,\,X_0X_3,\,Y_0Y_3,\,Z_0Z_3\} \]

can combine into a pure stabilizer on all four qubits is if they are – both the identity, or
– both the same non‐identity Pauli (so that \(E_{03}\,E_{21}=X_0X_3\,X_1X_2=S_X\), or similarly \(Z_0Z_3\,Z_1Z_2=S_Z\), or \(Y_0Y_3\,Y_1Y_2=S_XS_Z\)).
In all other undetectable‐error events the net effect is a non‐trivial logical operator \(L\neq I\) which maps the logical GHZ to an orthogonal code state and hence contributes zero fidelity.

  1. Probability of no logical error
    – Both errors are \(I\): probability \((1-p)^2\).
    – Both errors are the same non‐identity Pauli \(\alpha\in\{X,Y,Z\}\): each has prob.\ \((p/15)^2\), and there are 3 choices of \(\alpha\).
    Hence
\[ P_{\rm no\ logical} = (1-p)^2 \;+\;3\,\bigl(\tfrac p{15}\bigr)^{2} = (1-p)^2 \;+\;\frac{p^2}{75}\,. \]
  1. Conditional logical fidelity
    Because any surviving run with a non‐trivial logical \(L\) has zero overlap with the target GHZ, the post­selected logical fidelity is
\[ F_{\rm logical}(p) = \frac{P_{\rm no\ logical}}{P_{\rm pass}} = \frac{(1-p)^2 \;+\;\tfrac{p^2}{75}} {\bigl(1-\tfrac{4}{5}p\bigr)^2}\,. \]

Final Answer:

\[ \boxed{ F_{\rm logical}(p) = \frac{(1-p)^2 + \displaystyle\frac{p^2}{75}} {\bigl(1 - \tfrac{4}{5}\,p\bigr)^{2}}\,. } \]

Step‐by‐Step Derivation

We label the CNOT gates (in time from right to left) as G₂≡CNOT₁₂,\;G₃≡CNOT₁₀,\;G₄≡CNOT₂₃,\;G₅≡CNOT₃₄,\;G₆≡CNOT₀₄, and we append a two‐qubit depolarizing channel of strength p after each. The ancilla measurement (“M₄”) projects onto the +1‐eigenspace of the data operator

\[ Z_{0}Z_{3}\,, \]

so we post-select on runs in which no inserted Pauli errors ever flip that parity, and likewise we post–select on having no nontrivial code‐syndrome at the end. Under these hypotheses the only undetected errors that do degrade the logical |00〉₍AB₎ fidelity are those which act as a simultaneous bit–flip on both logical qubits, i.e.\ the logical operator

\[ X_{A}X_{B}=X_{1}X_{2} \]

(up to multiplication by stabilizers). Equivalently, on the four data qubits an undetected physical error must have exactly two X’s, either on {1,2} or (modulo the XXXX stabilizer) on {0,3}.

One checks by explicit propagation of Pauli errors through the tail end of the encoding circuit (see, e.g.\ Nielsen & Chuang §10.2) that the only pair of faulty gates which can conspire to produce such an undetected two-bit‐flip is the pair (G₅,G₆) that couples qubits 3→4→0. Concretely:

– An \(X\,\)–error on qubit 3 immediately after G₅ (\(CNOT_{34}\)), combined with
– an \(X\,\)–error on qubit 0 immediately after G₆ (\(CNOT_{04}\)),

propagates to a net data error \(X_{0}X_{3}\), which (i) commutes with \(Z_{0}Z_{3}\) (even flips at 0 and 3), (ii) commutes with the 4-qubit stabilizers \(XXXX\) and \(ZZZZ\), and (iii) is equivalent modulo the \(XXXX\)‐stabilizer to \(X_{1}X_{2}\), hence implements a logical \(X_{A}X_{B}\) that flips \(|00\>\to|11\>\).

All other single– or two–fault combinations either (a) anticommute with \(Z_{0}Z_{3}\) or with one of the code stabilizers (and so are detected and thrown away) or (b) act trivially on the logical \(|00\>_{AB}\) (pure phase or pure‐stabilizer) and so do not reduce the fidelity.

Since each two‐fault event \((G₅,G₆)\) with precisely an \(X_{3}\)‐error after G₅ and an \(X_{0}\)‐error after G₆ occurs with probability

\[ \frac p{15}\;\times\;\frac p{15} \]

and there are exactly 4 such Pauli–pairs (namely \(P_{34}\in\{X_{3}I_{4},\,X_{3}X_{4}\}\) and \(P_{04}\in\{X_{0}I_{4},\,X_{0}Z_{4}\}\),
with the other two ancilla‐Pauli factors fixed so as not to introduce any extra detected \(Z\)–errors), we find to second order in \(p\)

\[ P\bigl(\text{pass}\,\wedge\,\text{logical‐flip}\bigr) =4\;\Bigl(\tfrac p{15}\Bigr)^{2} =\frac{4}{225}\;p^{2} +O(p^{3})\,. \]

Meanwhile no single‐fault event yields an undetected logical bit–flip (that is the whole point of the fault‐tolerant design), so the only infidelity comes in at \(O(p^{2})\). Hence, to leading nontrivial order, the conditional logical fidelity is

\[ F_{\rm logical}(p) \;=\;1\;-\;P\bigl(\text{logical‐flip}\mid\text{pass}\bigr) \;=\;1\;-\;\frac{4}{225}\,p^{2}\;+\;O(p^{3})\,. \]

Final Answer:

\[ \boxed{% F_{\rm logical}(p)\;=\;1\;-\;\frac{4}{225}\,p^{2}\;+\;O(p^{3})\,.% } \]
