Problem setup:

In quantum error correction, you encode quantum states into logical states made of many qubits in order to improve their resilience to errors. In quantum error detection, you do the same but can only detect the presence of errors and not correct them. In this problem, we will consider a single [[4,2,2]] quantum error detection code, which encodes two logical qubits into four physical qubits, and investigate how robust logical quantum operations in this code are to quantum errors.

Our convention is that the four physical qubits in the [[4,2,2]] code are labelled 0,1,2,3. The two logical qubits are labelled A and B. The stabilizers are \(XXXX\) and \(ZZZZ\), where \(X\) and \(Z\) are Pauli matrices. The logical \(X\) and \(Z\) operators on the two qubits are \(X_A = XIXI\), \(X_B=XXII\), \(Z_A = ZZII\), \(Z_B = ZIZI\), up to multiplication by stabilizers.

We will consider different state preparation circuits consisting of controlled not \(CNOT_{ij}\) gates, where \(CNOT_{ij}\) has control qubit \(i\) and target qubit \(j\). As a simple model of quantum errors in hardware, we will suppose that each \(CNOT_{ij}\) gate in the circuit has a two qubit depolarizing error channel following it that produces one of the 15 non-identity two-qubit Paulis with equal probability \(p/15\). The probability \(p\) indicates the probability of an error in a single two-qubit gate. We will assess the logical infidelity of certain state preparation protocols as a function of the physical infidelity \(p\).

  • Stabilizers: \(S=\langle XXXX,\; ZZZZ\rangle\).

  • We post-select on runs with \(+1\) outcomes for both stabilizers (i.e., the error \(E\) must commute with both \(XXXX\) and \(ZZZZ\)).

  • Error model and independence across the two pairs

  • Two independent two-qubit depolarizing channels act:
    • After \(CNOT_{21}\) on qubits (2,1): error \(E_{21}\).
    • After \(CNOT_{03}\) on qubits (0,3): error \(E_{03}\).

  • No subsequent gates couple (2,1) to (0,3), so the total error is \(E=E_{21}\otimes E_{03}\).

  • Parity-classification of two-qubit Paulis on a pair

  • For a two-qubit Pauli \(P\in\{I,X,Y,Z\}^{\otimes 2}\) on a pair, define syndrome parities
\[ s_Z(P)=\#\{X,Y\}\bmod 2,\qquad s_X(P)=\#\{Z,Y\}\bmod 2, \]
i.e., whether $P$ anticommutes with $ZZ$ and $XX$, respectively.

  • This partitions the 16 Paulis on a pair into 4 classes labeled by \((s_Z,s_X)\):

    • \((0,0):\{II,XX,YY,ZZ\}\)
    • \((1,0):\{XI,IX,YZ,ZY\}\)
    • \((0,1):\{ZI,IZ,XY,YX\}\)
    • \((1,1):\{YI,IY,XZ,ZX\}\)

  • Acceptance (post-selection) condition

  • For \(E=E_{21}\otimes E_{03}\), the stabilizer outcomes are
\[ ZZZZ:\ (-1)^{s_Z(E_{21})\oplus s_Z(E_{03})},\qquad XXXX:\ (-1)^{s_X(E_{21})\oplus s_X(E_{03})}. \]

  • Accepted iff \(s_Z(E_{21})=s_Z(E_{03})\) and \(s_X(E_{21})=s_X(E_{03})\), i.e., both pair errors lie in the same parity class.

  • One-pair class probabilities under the two-qubit depolarizing channel

  • For one pair:
    • \(P(II)=1-p\),
    • each of the 15 non-identity Paulis has probability \(p/15\).
  • Therefore the probability a pair lands in each class is
\[ P_{00}=1-p+3\frac{p}{15}=1-\frac{12}{15}p,\qquad P_{10}=P_{01}=P_{11}=4\frac{p}{15}. \]
  • Accepted-event statistics and logical mapping
  • Because the two pair errors are independent, the probability to accept is
\[ P_{\mathrm{acc}}=\sum_{b\in\{00,10,01,11\}} P_b^2 =\left(1-\frac{12}{15}p\right)^2+3\left(\frac{4}{15}p\right)^2. \]
  • Conditioned on acceptance, both pairs are in the same Bell label \(b\). The resulting logical two-qubit state (on \(A,B\)) equals the logical Bell state with the same label \(b\):
    • \(b=00\ \Rightarrow\ |{\Phi^+}\rangle_{AB}\),
    • \(b=10\ \Rightarrow\ |{\Psi^+}\rangle_{AB}\),
    • \(b=01\ \Rightarrow\ |{\Phi^-}\rangle_{AB}\),
    • \(b=11\ \Rightarrow\ |{\Psi^-}\rangle_{AB}\), since \(X_AX_B=IXXI\) and \(Z_AZ_B=IZZI\) act as \(XX\) and \(ZZ\) on qubits (1,2).
  • Hence the logical state fidelity with the target \(|{\Phi^+}\rangle_{AB}\), conditioned on acceptance, is the conditional probability of \(b=00\):
\[ F_{\mathrm{logical}}(p) =\frac{P_{00}^2}{\sum_b P_b^2} =\frac{\left(1-\frac{12}{15}p\right)^2}{\left(1-\frac{12}{15}p\right)^2+3\left(\frac{4}{15}p\right)^2}. \]
  • Optional simplification
\[ F_{\mathrm{logical}}(p)=\frac{1-\frac{8}{5}p+\frac{16}{25}p^2}{1-\frac{8}{5}p+\frac{64}{75}p^2} =1-\frac{16}{75}p^2+O(p^3). \]
